// what is modbus alarm monitoring

Know the moment something goes wrong

Polling tells you the value now — but you cannot watch every register all the time. An alarm system watches for you and reacts the instant a limit is crossed.

ModbusManager Pro evaluates the registers and tags you choose on every poll cycle. When a value breaches a threshold, the alarm is raised, shown in colour, optionally sounded, and recorded in history. With warning and critical levels, hysteresis and delay, you get meaningful alerts instead of a flood of noise — all inside the same application, with no separate SCADA or alarm server.

// how to set up modbus alarms

Three steps to live alerts

Define once, and the alarm system watches every poll cycle.

Step 1
Choose a source
Pick a register (slave, function code, address) or a named tag as the value to monitor. RTU and TCP devices are both supported.
Step 2
Set limits
Choose a condition and set warning and critical limits. Add hysteresis and an activation delay to keep alerts clean, and enable sound if you want it.
Step 3
Monitor & acknowledge
Active alarms appear in the Alarms tab and on the dashboard. Acknowledge them as you handle each one, and review the full history any time.

// frequently asked questions

Modbus alarms — FAQ

Can ModbusManager raise alarms on register values?+
Yes. ModbusManager Pro includes an alarm system. You define alarms on registers or tags with conditions such as greater than, less than, equal, between or outside a range, and the app raises an alarm when the condition is met.
Does it support warning and critical alarm levels?+
Yes. Each alarm can have a warning level and a critical level. An active alarm escalates automatically from warning to critical as the value worsens, shown in different colours.
How do I stop alarms from flickering near the limit?+
Each alarm supports hysteresis and an activation delay. Hysteresis requires the value to move back past the limit by a set amount before the alarm clears, and the delay ignores momentary spikes.
Can I acknowledge alarms and see their history?+
Yes. Active alarms can be acknowledged from the Alarms tab or directly on the dashboard. A full history records when each alarm was raised, acknowledged and cleared.
Do alarms make a sound?+
Yes. Alarms can play an audible alert with different tones for warning and critical, either repeating until acknowledged or a single beep, with a global mute. Sound can be enabled per alarm.
Is the alarm system in the free Standard edition?+
The alarm system is a Pro-edition feature. ModbusManager Standard ($49) covers polling, slave mode and simulation; alarms and the Historian are part of Pro ($119).
